Calorific Value of Fuels




What does Calorific Value mean?

Calorific value is the amount of heat energy present in food or fuel, determined by the complete combustion of specified quantity at constant pressure and in normal conditions. It is also called calorific power.


Units and Calculations

The calorific value of fuels is usually measured in:
  • Joules (j/g)
  • Kilojoules (kJ/kg)
